Our Programs & Impact

Making a Real Difference
in Learners' Lives

We provide a meaningful "final push" by addressing both visible and invisible barriers. Our programs are designed to ensure learners are not only able to write their exams, but are supported to transition successfully into what comes next.

Our Approach

Four Pillars of Support

We address the real challenges facing matriculants through comprehensive, learner-centred programs.

Academic Guidance

We provide practical resources and study guides tailored for the South African matric curriculum, helping students navigate the academic pressure of final examinations.

  • Past exam papers & memo guides
  • Subject-specific study materials
  • One-on-one tutoring sessions
  • Exam preparation workshops

Emotional Wellbeing

Matric is stressful. We offer support structures to manage the mental and emotional toll, building the resilience needed to complete schooling with confidence.

  • Mental health counseling
  • Stress management workshops
  • Peer support groups
  • Crisis intervention support

Mentorship Program

Learners are matched with caring mentors who provide guidance, encouragement, and real-world insights into life after matric.

  • One-on-one mentor matching
  • Career guidance & planning
  • Life skills development
  • Goal setting & accountability
Why We Exist

Closing the Opportunity Gap

Final Push Foundation exists because of the growing gap between pass rates and real-world challenges. We prioritize learners from under-resourced communities, responding to real needs—not assumptions—to ensure every student completes matric with dignity and hope.

The Barriers We Address

Lack of Basic Resources

Many learners lack pens, calculators, textbooks, and other essential study materials.

Limited Academic Support

Access to tutoring, study groups, and academic guidance is often unavailable in under-resourced communities.

Emotional & Mental Stress

The pressure of matric, combined with personal challenges, creates significant emotional burden.

Uncertainty About the Future

Many learners lack guidance on post-school pathways, bursaries, and career opportunities.

Providing the meaningful "final push" for South African matriculants to complete their education with dignity, resilience, and hope for the future.
